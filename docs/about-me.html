Hello there! My name is Conner Harbaugh.

As an Eagle Scout, I work towards creating and giving back to the commuunity. It is partially why I am also currently majoring in Computer Science at the University of Central Florida as I see computer science as a unique way to give back and help other people through innovative and accessible programs and software. The other reason that got me into computer science was my interest at a young age when I participated and competed with my high school's cyber security club.

Competing and learing at my cybersecurity club in high school taught me a lot of valuable things. One is that it got me experience with using a lot of tools such as Linux, Amazon Web Services, web applications, and much much more. This exposure helped spark interest in the computer field. I have also gained great soft skills through competing in the competions as a team. Durning the competitions while under the threat of live hackers it can be immensely stressful and being able to work together is key. Having won two out of three times at the Palmetto Cyber Defense Competition, it seems that the teamwork we had paid off and those skills I gained with communication and teamwork will last a life time.

These soft skills where further carried along when I worked on my capstone project for high school. There I created and ran a cyber security competiton amongst members of other schools to compete against each other. This used live enviroments where I had to lead and build out various machines to stimulate a business like network using VMware ESXI to spin up machines such as web applications via LAMP stack, ftp servers, remote desktop and AD servers. The whole capstone process involved using new technologies I have used for such as Microsoft Project to help keep track of the progress. It again involved soft skills where I have to communicate to the people that I was working with so that we could stay on pace and accomplish our goals.

This exprience and skills have landed me my current job as a webdeveloper and techranger for the University of Central Florida's Center for Distrubuted Learning. There I apply my knowledge and skill set to develop and help maintain the university's online learning projects which are used by more than thousands of students each day. This has begun to help teach me industry level pratices as well as improving my skills in software development which I can then carry and apply to in real life and other jobs.

Hello there. My name is Conner Harbaugh and I designed this website. As someone who is passionate about computers, programming is just one of the many ways to show off my skills. One of these competitions was the Air Force Association's CyberPatriot competition, which I did in middle school and high school, where my team and I made it in the top of our state multiple times. Another competition I competed in in high school was the Palmetto Cyber Defense Competition (PCDC) which is a competition that involves securing networks of machines designed to stimulate normal business applications from hackers attacking in real time. My team and I managed to win twice out of the three times we competed. Today I still participate in cyber competitions with my college such as various Capture the Flag competitions and the National Colliegate Cyber Defense competition.

I am also an Eagle Scout, the highest rank in the Boy Scouts of America awarded for leadership and knowlege of the outdoors. Taking two days it was diffently a good learning expirence not only for the leadership skills as I had to organize it but also nice to see how it helped out the community looking at how well the posts came out.
