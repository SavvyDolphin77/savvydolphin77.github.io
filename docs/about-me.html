About Me

Hello there. My name is Conner Harbaugh and I designed this website. As someone who is passionate about computers, programming is just one of the many ways to show off my skills. This passion for computers came about around 7th grade where I participated in my high school's cyber security club. There I gained knowledge on how to keep computers safe, use various software to set up secure applications like websites and databases, and even got to compete in some competitions. One of these competitions was the Air Force Association's CyberPatriot competition, which I did in middle school and high school, where my team and I made it in the top of our state multiple times. Another competition I competed in in high school was the Palmetto Cyber Defense Competition (PCDC) which is a competition that involves securing networks of machines designed to stimulate normal business applications from hackers attacking in real time. My team and I managed to win twice out of the three times we competed. Today I still participate in cyber competitions with my college such as various Capture the Flag competitions and the National Colliegate Cyber Defense competition.

I am also an Eagle Scout, the highest rank in the Boy Scouts of America awarded for leadership and knowlege of the outdoors. To obtain this presitigious award I had to manage and led a community service project. The one that I did was to help refurbish all 170 street posts of Sullivan's Island in South Carolina. There I and a team of volunteers that I got together worked to scrub down and repaint each of the posts. Taking two days it was diffently a good learning expirence not only for the leadership skills as I had to organize it but also nice to see how it helped out the community looking at how well the posts came out.

Another project I lead was my capstone for my senior year in High School. My capstone was essentially a project to run an organize a cyber security competition similar to that of the Palmetto Cyber Defense Competition and National Colliegate Cyber Defense Competition. I worked with my school's cyber security team and used business tools such as Microsoft Project to plan and organize the effots to create the competition setup. There I had to plan out and create a business like network by creating LAMP stacks, web applications, databases, file servers, and more to then add vulernabilities to them. It definitely helped grow my understanding of the technology and increase my interest in using these tools.
